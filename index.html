Research Highlights

I'm on my mission to build our robotic friends 🤖 to enable robots and agents to act autonomously in the open world, to land in an unseen environment, infer what their user wants, respect the constraints they're under, and figure out how to act, all at runtime.

My long-term interest is to establish a robot-learning paradigm where capabilities are not hard-coded at training time but assembled and steered at runtime, and continuously sharpened by the robot's own deployment experience, history, and memory.

My research focuses on inference-time compute: composing capabilities to satisfy multiple objectives at runtime — task, human intent, environment, safety. My work spans diffusion-model guidance (CAR), generalisable skill learning (SaMI), and fast online adaptation (ARLPE); together, I believe these works can offer some insights into trustworthy deployed autonomy.

I advocate for robots that are capable by training, steerable by inference, and improved by experience.

I'm open to research discussions and collaborations — please feel free to get in touch!
